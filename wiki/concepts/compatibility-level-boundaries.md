# Compatibility Level Boundaries

## Status

This page is a **project heuristic**, not canonical doctrine of Socionics, Psychosophy, or Temporistics.

The project uses three typologies because it models three different latent processes. The typologies should not be collapsed into one another.

## Short Definition

| Level | Core question | Latent process | Frame type | Primary typology |
|-------|---------------|----------------|------------|------------------|
| **Strategic** | Why and where are we going? | Inductive-deductive structuring of temporal experience | Temporal frame | **Temporistics** |
| **Operational** | What do we prioritize and how do we organize action? | Analysis, synthesis, and action organization | Action frame | **Psychosophy** |
| **Tactical** | How do we perceive, interpret, and exchange situation information? | Information modeling / information metabolism | Information frame | **Socionics** |

Compact rule:

- **Strategic = same road?**
- **Operational = can we run life together?**
- **Tactical = do our minds/interface patterns fit?**

## Boundary Rules

### Rule 1: Each level has one primary typology

Compatibility scores should use the level's primary typology as the main scoring source:

- Strategic score -> **Temporistics**
- Operational score -> **Psychosophy**
- Tactical score -> **Socionics**

Other systems may add nuance, but should not replace the primary typology for that level.

### Rule 2: Secondary systems are annotations, not translations

Secondary signals may be used for:

- confidence adjustment
- explanation
- contradiction flags
- risk caveats
- hypothesis generation

Secondary systems should not be used to directly translate one typology into another.

### Rule 3: Similar words are not the same construct

False equivalences to avoid:

| False equivalence | Correct boundary |
|-------------------|------------------|
| Psychosophy Emotion = Socionics Fe/Fi | Emotion is an action-priority domain; Fe/Fi are ethical information elements |
| Psychosophy Will = Socionics Se | Will is agency/goal-pressure stance; Se is information about force/impact/control |
| Psychosophy Logic = Socionics Ti/Te | Logic is relation to reasoning/opinions in action; Ti/Te are logical information elements |
| Psychosophy Physics = Socionics Si | Physics is material-domain priority and handling; Si is sensory-comfort information processing |
| Temporistics Future = Socionics Ni | Future is temporal life trajectory; Ni is information modeling of time/development |
| Temporistics Present = Socionics Si/Se | Present is place/current-life frame; Si/Se are sensory information aspects |
| Temporistics Eternity = Socionics Fi/Ni | Eternity is meaning/existential continuity frame, not one information element |

## 1. Strategic Level

Strategic compatibility measures whether two people can inhabit compatible long-horizon temporal and meaning frames.

Use **Temporistics** when the question is about:

- life direction
- temporal horizon
- meaning / existential frame
- identity continuity through time
- relationship to past, present, future, and eternity

Do **not** use the strategic level to score:

- communication style
- fine-grained conflict behavior
- daily task delegation
- Model A information exchange

Common error:

> “Strong Ni means strategic compatibility.”

Correction: Socionics Ni models time/development as information. Temporistics Future/Eternity describe temporal worldview and meaning orientation.

## 2. Operational Level

Operational compatibility measures whether two people can organize action together.

Use **Psychosophy** when the question is about:

- role distribution in action
- who leads, supports, hesitates, or accepts a frame
- friction around Will, Emotion, Logic, and Physics
- how people handle pressure, reasoning, emotional tone, and material life
- day-to-day execution compatibility

Do **not** use the operational level to score:

- information metabolism itself
- life mission itself
- Socionics intertype relation as such
- temporal worldview by itself

Common error:

> “1E means strong Fe.”

Correction: Psychosophy 1E means Emotion is a primary operational material. Socionics Fe is information about emotional atmosphere and expression. See [[emotion-vs-ethics-boundary]].

## 3. Tactical Level

Tactical compatibility measures whether two people naturally exchange and interpret situation information in mutually supportive ways.

Use **Socionics** when the question is about:

- information exchange fit
- how people read and model situations
- communication/comprehension style
- where one partner feeds another's weak/valued functions
- where one partner hits another's vulnerable functions
- intertype relation quality in Model A

Do **not** use the tactical level to score:

- deep life meaning
- action priorities as such
- Psychosophy role distribution
- long-term temporal worldview

Common error:

> “Good everyday compatibility means Socionics duality.”

Correction: everyday smoothness may come from Psychosophy, shared context, habits, or practical arrangements. Socionics should be used for information exchange, not every kind of comfort.

## Surface Behavior Is Not Enough

The same observed behavior can come from different latent processes.

Example: “The person avoids making a decision.”

Possible sources:

- **Socionics:** they are missing or mistrusting a type of information
- **Psychosophy:** Will/Logic position makes action-frame assembly difficult
- **Temporistics:** Future position makes trajectory or consequence feel unsafe

Therefore, never type or score by a single surface behavior. Ask:

1. What information is being processed? -> **Socionics**
2. How is action/pressure/role being organized? -> **Psychosophy**
3. What temporal/meaning frame makes this important? -> **Temporistics**

## Evidence Routing Rules

| Evidence is mainly about... | Route first to | Primary typology |
|-----------------------------|----------------|------------------|
| “What did they notice, miss, or misread?” | Tactical | Socionics |
| “Who decided, pushed, yielded, assembled, or accepted?” | Operational | Psychosophy |
| “What does this mean for identity, place, future, or purpose?” | Strategic | Temporistics |

## Secondary Signal Policy

| Use case | Allowed? | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Confidence adjustment | Yes | e.g. primary score is clear but another level predicts instability |
| Explanatory annotation | Yes | “tactically easy, strategically unstable” |
| Contradiction flag | Yes | useful for caveats |
| Direct score substitution | No | primary typology must remain primary |
| Literal translation across systems | No | constructs are non-equivalent |
| Averaging same-named constructs | No | causes construct collapse |

## Recommended Compatibility Output Pattern

For any pair analysis:

1. **Strategic score** from Temporistics
2. **Operational score** from Psychosophy
3. **Tactical score** from Socionics
4. Then add:
   - reinforcing signals
   - contradictions between levels
   - risk flags
   - confidence estimate

## Classic Mismatch Patterns

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| High tactical, low strategic | Easy conversation, different life arc |
| High strategic, low tactical | Same direction, repeated misunderstanding |
| High strategic, low operational | Same destination, poor joint execution |
| High operational, low strategic | Runs well day-to-day, but lacks shared long-term meaning |
| High tactical, low operational | Good chemistry, exhausting decisions and role distribution |

## Relation Names Caveat

Do not import relation names from one system into another as if they were canonical.

Especially in Temporistics, terms like “dual-like”, “activation-like”, or “conflict-like” should be marked as **project analogies** unless backed by a specific Temporistics source.

## Activity Theory Caveat

The project uses the labels **strategic / operational / tactical** as an internal compatibility model.

These labels should not be confused with Activity Theory terminology, where “operation”, “action”, and “activity” may map differently. In this project:

- Tactical -> Socionics
- Operational -> Psychosophy
- Strategic -> Temporistics

## Practical Shorthand

- **Socionics:** “What signal do you carry, and how do you read mine?”
- **Psychosophy:** “Who assembles the decision, and who pressures whom?”
- **Temporistics:** “For what purpose, in what life-time, is this happening?”
