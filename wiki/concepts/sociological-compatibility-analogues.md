# Sociological Compatibility Analogues

## Status

This page maps the project's **Strategic / Operational / Tactical** compatibility heuristic to adjacent constructs in sociology and social science.

These are **analogies and bridges**, not direct validations of Socionics, Psychosophy, or Temporistics.

Sociology is used here as an **external context layer**:

- to generate realistic relationship and role-fit scenarios
- to identify social variables that moderate typology-based hypotheses
- to prevent over-attributing outcomes to typology when class, gender, institutions, labor markets, war, or household structure may explain them better

## Core Mapping

| Project level | Typology layer | Sociological bridge | Strength of analogy |
|---------------|----------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Strategic** | Temporistics | life course, shared meaning, value/institutional scripts, assortative mating | strong |
| **Operational** | Psychosophy | role theory, social exchange, household labor, resource bargaining | strong |
| **Tactical** | Socionics | symbolic interactionism, interaction rituals, ethnomethodology, conversation analysis | medium-strong |

Compact formula:

```text
Strategic compatibility
= compatible life trajectories + temporal worldview + shared meaning horizon

Operational compatibility
= compatible role allocation + resource exchange + pressure/action coordination

Tactical compatibility
= compatible situated sense-making + information exchange + interaction repair
```

## Strategic Analogues

Strategic compatibility asks whether two people share a compatible life arc, meaning horizon, and temporal orientation.

### Life Course Theory

**Core idea:** relationships develop inside life trajectories, transitions, turning points, historical context, age timing, and linked lives.

Useful concepts:

- trajectories
- transitions
- turning points
- timing of lives
- linked lives
- cohort and historical context

Authors / search anchors:

- Glen H. Elder Jr.
- Tamara Hareven
- Karl Ulrich Mayer
- Michael Shanahan
- `life course theory relationships`
- `linked lives marriage`
- `life trajectories family sociology`

Project use:

- strong bridge to Temporistics
- helps model “same road?” questions
- useful for scenarios like relocation, marriage timing, children, war disruption, career transition, migration

Limit:

- life course theory is not a typology of temporal psyche; it studies trajectories and institutions.

### Assortative Mating / Homogamy

**Core idea:** people often form relationships with others similar in education, class, religion, age, values, lifestyle, or cultural capital.

Useful concepts:

- educational homogamy
- value homogamy
- religious homogamy
- class homogamy
- cultural matching
- status matching

Authors / search anchors:

- Matthijs Kalmijn
- Christine Schwartz
- Robert Mare
- Pierre Bourdieu as background for cultural capital
- `assortative mating values`
- `educational homogamy marriage`
- `status homogamy partner choice`

Project use:

- provides population-level context for strategic matching
- helps distinguish “shared life path” from type compatibility

Limit:

- explains who tends to pair with whom statistically; does not explain micro-interaction fit.

### Symbolic Construction Of Marriage

**Core idea:** couples construct a shared reality through conversation, routines, social recognition, and shared narratives.

Useful sources:

- Peter Berger and Hansfried Kellner, “Marriage and the Construction of Reality”
- symbolic interactionism
- institutional scripts of marriage/family

Project use:

- bridge between Temporistics and sociology of meaning
- useful for strategic compatibility as shared narrative construction

## Operational Analogues

Operational compatibility asks whether two people can organize action, roles, pressure, labor, and resources together.

### Role Theory

**Core idea:** social life is organized by roles, expectations, role conflict, role strain, and role-taking.

Useful concepts:

- role expectations
- role conflict
- role strain
- role overload
- family roles
- role-taking

Authors / search anchors:

- Ralph Linton
- Robert K. Merton
- Talcott Parsons
- Robert Bales
- William Goode
- Bruce Biddle
- `role theory family sociology`
- `role strain marriage`
- `family role expectations`

Project use:

- strong bridge to Psychosophy as action-role compatibility
- useful for modeling who leads, supports, yields, manages, negotiates, or absorbs responsibility

Limit:

- social roles may be imposed by gender, class, institutions, or economics, not by typological action priorities.

### Social Exchange / Interdependence Theory

**Core idea:** relationships involve resources, costs, rewards, alternatives, dependence, power, fairness, and commitment.

Useful concepts:

- costs and rewards
- comparison level
- alternatives
- dependence
- power-dependence
- investment and commitment

Authors / search anchors:

- George Homans
- Peter Blau
- John Thibaut
- Harold Kelley
- Richard Emerson
- Caryl Rusbult
- `social exchange theory intimate relationships`
- `interdependence theory relationships`
- `investment model commitment Rusbult`

Project use:

- bridge to operational compatibility through resource exchange and pressure handling
- useful for money, care, labor, commitment, sacrifice, and fairness scenarios

Limit:

- can be too rationalistic; does not fully capture meaning, identity, or information metabolism.

### Household Labor / Division Of Labor

**Core idea:** shared life depends on distribution of domestic tasks, care work, emotional labor, mental load, financial responsibility, and invisible work.

Useful concepts:

- household labor
- second shift
- emotional labor
- mental load
- care work
- doing gender

Authors / search anchors:

- Arlie Hochschild
- Anne Machung
- Candace West
- Don Zimmerman
- Scott Coltrane
- Paula England
- Cecilia Ridgeway
- `household labor relationship satisfaction`
- `division of labor marriage`
- `second shift Hochschild`
- `mental load relationships`

Project use:

- one of the strongest empirical bridges to operational compatibility
- provides realistic scenarios: cleaning, budgeting, illness, children, elder care, relocation, work-life balance

Limit:

- not a personality model; it reveals social patterns, inequalities, and negotiation structures.

## Tactical Analogues

Tactical compatibility asks whether people can understand, repair, and synchronize interaction in the moment.

### Symbolic Interactionism

**Core idea:** people act based on meanings, and meanings are created and revised through interaction.

Useful concepts:

- definition of the situation
- role-taking
- identity negotiation
- shared symbols
- interpretation of gestures

Authors / search anchors:

- George Herbert Mead
- Herbert Blumer
- Erving Goffman
- `symbolic interactionism intimate relationships`
- `definition of the situation relationships`

Project use:

- bridge to tactical meaning-making and misreading
- useful for modeling how partners interpret the same event differently

Limit:

- process theory, not Model A or stable information-function typology.

### Interaction Ritual Theory

**Core idea:** interaction creates or drains emotional energy through mutual focus, rhythm, shared symbols, and solidarity.

Useful concepts:

- mutual focus
- emotional energy
- ritual chains
- interaction order
- face-work

Authors / search anchors:

- Erving Goffman
- Randall Collins
- `interaction ritual chains relationships`
- `emotional energy interaction rituals`
- `Goffman interaction order intimacy`

Project use:

- bridge to moment-to-moment contact quality
- useful for “this interaction energizes/drains us” scenarios

Limit:

- does not distinguish information elements as Socionics does.

### Ethnomethodology / Conversation Analysis

**Core idea:** social order is produced in detailed interaction: turn-taking, repair, adjacency pairs, accountability, and shared methods of sense-making.

Useful concepts:

- turn-taking
- repair sequences
- adjacency pairs
- misunderstanding and clarification
- accountability in conversation

Authors / search anchors:

- Harold Garfinkel
- Harvey Sacks
- Emanuel Schegloff
- Gail Jefferson
- `conversation analysis repair relationships`
- `turn taking intimate relationships`
- `ethnomethodology sense making interaction`

Project use:

- strong empirical bridge to tactical compatibility
- can provide observable markers for interaction simulations

Limit:

- conversation analysis usually avoids internal psychological typologies; it analyzes visible interaction practices.

## Scenario Design Implications

Sociology can be used as an external scenario ontology.

| Scenario | Sociological frame | Project level |
|----------|--------------------|---------------|
| Moving for one partner's career | life course / linked lives | Strategic |
| Different expectations of marriage | institutional scripts / symbolic interactionism | Strategic |
| Who handles care work during illness | household labor / role theory | Operational |
| Conflict over money and dependence | social exchange / power-dependence | Operational |
| Argument after an ambiguous message | conversation analysis / symbolic interactionism | Tactical |
| Repeated failure to repair misunderstandings | conversation analysis / interaction ritual theory | Tactical |
| Feeling drained by daily interaction rhythm | interaction ritual theory | Tactical |
| Mismatch between status/class expectations | assortative mating / stratification | Strategic + Operational |

## Strong Analogies

| Bridge | Why it is strong |
|--------|------------------|
| Temporistics ↔ life course theory | both deal with time, trajectories, transitions, past/future orientation, and life direction |
| Psychosophy ↔ role theory / household labor | both deal with action, responsibility, pressure, and practical life organization |
| Socionics ↔ symbolic interactionism / conversation analysis | both concern interpretation, communication, misunderstanding, and repair |
| Three-level model ↔ activity/action/operation | provides a why/what/how metaframework, with caveats |

## Weak Analogies And Caveats

| Caveat | Why it matters |
|--------|----------------|
| Sociology does not validate the typologies directly | do not claim academic sociology proves Socionics, Psychosophy, or Temporistics |
| Sociology explains social structure, norms, and practices | typologies model latent individual/interaction processes |
| Tactical sociology is not Model A | conversation analysis studies observable conversation, not cognitive function positions |
| Strategic sociology may be macro-social | class, religion, education, and cohort can mimic “shared meaning” without being temporal worldview |
| Operational sociology may be structurally imposed | household roles may come from gender, income, war, or institutions rather than Psychosophy |

## Practical Use In The Matcher

Use sociological analogues to add non-typological variables:

- education and class homogamy
- religious/value scripts
- age and life-stage timing
- migration and war disruption
- division of labor expectations
- care-work burden
- economic dependence
- occupational culture
- communication repair patterns
- institutional constraints

These should be modeled as **moderators**, not type indicators.

Example:

```text
Typology hypothesis:
  operational friction around Will/Physics

Sociological moderator:
  household labor inequality + gender script + income dependence

Better explanation:
  not just type mismatch; practical burden and institutional expectations amplify the mismatch
```
