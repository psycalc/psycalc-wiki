# Psychology of Everything

## Definition

“Psychology of everything” is not a standard academic field. In PsyCalc it is used as a loose umbrella for attempts to build a broad map of mind, behavior, personality, cognition, meaning, relationships, and social context.

The closest established search terms are:

- **unified psychology**
- **grand unified theory of psychology**
- **integrative psychology**
- **metatheory of psychology**
- **theoretical psychology**
- **consilience**
- **unified theories of cognition**

## Main intellectual clusters

### Unified psychology and metatheory

Unified psychology projects try to reduce fragmentation across psychological subfields by proposing higher-level organizing principles. They often ask how cognition, personality, emotion, behavior, development, society, and culture can be described in one coherent framework.

### Cognitive architectures

Frameworks such as ACT-R and SOAR attempt to model cognition as an integrated architecture. They are relevant to a “psychology of everything” because they show how memory, attention, learning, and problem solving can be modeled together, though they do not cover all of personality, meaning, or social life.

### Predictive processing and active inference

Predictive processing and active inference are broad neurocognitive frameworks that describe perception, action, attention, interoception, and learning through prediction and error minimization. They are useful as mechanism-adjacent context, but they should not be treated as direct validation of typology.

### Global workspace and consciousness frameworks

Global Workspace Theory explains conscious access as information becoming globally available to many specialized systems. It is a powerful integrative theory of consciousness and attention, but not a total psychology of personality or compatibility.

### Sociology of “maps of everything”

Online communities often create and circulate totalizing maps because they compress complexity, create shared language, and help people orient identity, relationships, work, and meaning. This explains why typologies, integral theories, and personality maps spread quickly online.

## Similarity to PsyCalc

PsyCalc shares several motivations with unified psychology:

- it tries to explain surface behavior through hidden processes;
- it uses multiple levels of analysis;
- it treats isolated typological labels as insufficient;
- it tries to build bridges between personality, compatibility, roles, and simulation.

In PsyCalc’s current formula:

- **Socionics → latent processes of information modeling**
- **Psychosophy → latent processes of synthesis and analysis in action**
- **Temporistics → latent processes of induction and deduction in temporal/existential experience**

## Difference from PsyCalc

PsyCalc should not be framed as a completed universal psychology. It is narrower and more provisional:

- it starts from typological systems rather than from a consensus empirical ontology;
- it focuses on compatibility architecture, role fit, team design, and simulation;
- its mappings are project hypotheses, not established scientific facts;
- it still needs operationalization, measurement, validation, and calibration.

## Caveats

Large “psychology of everything” frameworks are useful as maps, but risky when treated as proof.

Common failure modes:

- turning metaphors into entities;
- explaining every domain with one vocabulary;
- ignoring social institutions and context;
- using beautiful coherence instead of measurement;
- becoming unfalsifiable;
- making clinical, theological, career, military, or relationship claims without the required evidence.

## PsyCalc positioning

Safe positioning:

> PsyCalc is not a final psychology of everything. It is a research framework that uses typologies as heuristic compressed hypotheses about latent processes, then asks whether those hypotheses can be operationalized, caveated, and validated.

This keeps PsyCalc closer to a testable research program than to a closed totalizing system.
