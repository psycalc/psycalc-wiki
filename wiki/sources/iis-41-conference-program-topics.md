## PsyCalc-Authored Summaries

### PsyCalc summary: Socionics, MBTI, and Big Five

The conference program lists a talk by **A. V. Bukalov** comparing Socionics, Jungian typology, MBTI, and Big Five in terms of claimed psychophysiological correlates.

**Safe use:** cite this as evidence that IIS/Bukalov is discussing a comparative typology-and-baseline question. Do not cite the program as evidence that Socionics or MBTI have established psychophysiological correlates, or that Big Five lacks such correlates.

### PsyCalc summary: Improving Typing Accuracy

The program lists a talk by **Yu. P. Minaev** on improving typing accuracy as practitioners master additional bipolar type traits.

**Safe use:** cite this as evidence that typing accuracy and additional diagnostic traits are active topics in the IIS conference ecosystem. Do not cite the program as proof that any specific trait set improves accuracy without a separate article, method, dataset, or validation study.

### PsyCalc summary: AI Hallucinations from a Socionics Model Perspective

The program lists a talk by **A. V. Bukalov** on the problem of AI hallucinations from the perspective of a Socionics model.

**Safe use:** cite this as evidence that some Socionics authors are beginning to apply Socionics concepts to AI behavior and LLM reliability problems. Do not cite the program as evidence that Socionics explains AI hallucinations unless a substantive paper or dataset is available.

## PsyCalc Relevance

These topics are relevant to PsyCalc because they touch three project concerns:

- comparison of Socionics with mainstream personality baselines such as Big Five;
- reliability and calibration of typing procedures;
- use and limits of typological models when interpreting AI behavior.

For PsyCalc, they should be treated as **topic signals** and **research leads**, not as validated findings.
