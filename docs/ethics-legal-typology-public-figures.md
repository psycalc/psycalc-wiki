# Ethics and Legal Framework: Typology of Public Figures

## Overview

This document addresses the ethical and legal implications of assigning typological profiles (Temporistics, Psychosophy, Socionics) to public figures without their consent.

---

## 1. Legal Analysis

### 1.1 Right of Publicity

The **right of publicity** is a tort protecting an individual's identity from unauthorized commercial exploitation.

**Elements Required** (US law, ~36 states):
1. Use of plaintiff's identity
2. Appropriation to defendant's advantage (commercial or otherwise)
3. Lack of consent
4. Resulting injury

**Application to Typology:**
- Typology assignment is NOT commercial use (typically)
- It's opinion/analysis, not identity exploitation
- Generally protected as free expression

### 1.2 Privacy Law

| Type | Applies to Typology? | Notes |
|------|---------------------|-------|
| Intrusion upon seclusion | No | Typology is public info |
| Public disclosure of private facts | No | Public figures have reduced expectation |
| False light | **Maybe** | If representation is misleading |
| Appropriation | No | Non-commercial use |

### 1.3 Defamation

**Important**: Typology is interpretation, not fact.

- "Залужний — IEI" = opinion, like astrology
- Must be able to prove false statement of fact
- Opinion is protected speech

**Exception**: If typology causes demonstrable harm to reputation in specific context (e.g., employment)

### 1.4 GDPR / Data Protection (EU)

Under GDPR, personality typing could be considered **profiling**:

> "Any form of automated processing of personal data to evaluate, analyze, or predict personal aspects concerning an identified or identifiable natural person"

**Legitimate Interest** exceptions:
- Publicly available information only
- Opinion expression (Art. 85)
- Scientific/historical research (Art. 85)

### 1.5 UK-Specific

From ICO guidance on profiling in political campaigning:

> "Using psychometric profiling to target individuals... could be considered unfair and therefore in breach of UK GDPR"

**Key distinction**: Political targeting = unfair. Academic analysis = potentially acceptable.

### 1.6 Washington State Law (2026)

New law (effective June 2026) defines "Forged Digital Likeness" — but requires:
- "Indistinguishable from genuine depiction"
- "Misrepresents the person's appearance, speech, or conduct"

Typology assignment does NOT misrepresent — it's interpretation.

## 2. Ethical Analysis

### 2.1 The Goldwater Rule

**APA Principle**: Psychiatrists/psychologists should NOT offer professional opinions about public figures they have not examined.

**Key Requirements**:
- Must clarify it's NOT a diagnosis
- Must state examination was not conducted
- Must explain limitations
- Must base on publicly available information

**Our Position**: We are NOT psychiatrists (no clinical claim). This is typological analysis, not psychological diagnosis.

### 2.2 APA Ethics Code

**Standard 9.01** — Bases for Assessments:

> "Psychologists provide opinions of the psychological characteristics of individuals only after they have conducted an examination of the individuals adequate to support their statements or conclusions."

**Exception** (9.01b):
> "When psychologists conduct a record review or provide consultation... and an individual examination is not warranted or necessary... psychologists explain this and the sources of information on which they base their opinions."

**Applying to Our Case**:
- We explicitly state no examination was conducted
- We explain limitations
- We base on publicly available information only
- We clarify it's opinion, not diagnosis

### 2.3 Ethical vs. Legal

| Issue | Legal Risk | Ethical Risk |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Typology without consent | Low (opinion) | Moderate (professional standards) |
| Public figure | Very Low | Moderate |
| Private individual | **Higher** | **High** |
| Commercial use | **High** | **High** |

## 3. Guidelines for Responsible Typology

### 3.1 When It's Acceptable

| Condition | Public Figure | Private Individual |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Based on public statements | ✅ | ✅ (with caveats) |
| Opinion, not diagnosis | ✅ | ⚠️ |
| Non-commercial use | ✅ | ⚠️ |
| Clear disclaimer | Required | Required |
| Commercial use | ⚠️ | ❌ |

### 3.2 Mandatory Disclaimers

Every typology of a public figure must include:

```
DISCLAIMER: This typology is an interpretation based on publicly 
available information only. It does not constitute a psychological 
diagnosis. No examination was conducted. The subject has not 
consented to this analysis. This is opinion, not fact.
```

### 3.3 What We Should NOT Do

- ❌ Claim clinical diagnosis
- ❌ Use for commercial purposes without consent
- ❌ Target private individuals
- ❌ Make definitive predictions about behavior
- ❌ Present as established science

### 3.4 What We CAN Do

- ✅ Academic research on typology methods
- ✅ Analysis of public figures for methodology testing
- ✅ Opinion expressed clearly as opinion
- ✅ Historical/public figure case studies
- ✅ Use with explicit disclaimers

---

## 4. Best Practices

### 4.1 For Public Figures

1. **Only use publicly available information**
2. **Clearly label as "analysis/opinion"**
3. **State no examination was conducted**
4. **Include explicit disclaimer**
5. **Don't claim predictive accuracy**
6. **Don't use for commercial purposes**

### 4.2 For Private Individuals

**DO NOT type without explicit consent.** The legal and ethical risks are significantly higher.

### 4.3 For Research

Use public figures for:
- Methodology validation
- Testing typology consistency
- Academic papers with proper disclosure

### 4.4 For Product (Future)

If building a product:
- Obtain explicit consent
- Allow opt-out / deletion
- Don't use for targeting/advertising
- Comply with GDPR/CCPA

## 5. Summary Matrix

| Scenario | Legal OK | Ethical OK | Notes |
|----------|---------|------------|-------|
| Academic analysis of public figure | ✅ | ✅ | With disclaimer |
| Typology without consent (public) | ✅ | ⚠️ | Keep non-commercial |
| Clinical diagnosis claim | ❌ | ❌ | Never do this |
| Private individual typing | ⚠️ | ❌ | Need consent |
| Commercial use | ⚠️ | ❌ | Need consent + contract |
| Prediction of behavior | ❌ | ❌ | Never claim this |

---

## 6. Key Takeaways

1. **Typology is opinion, not fact** — Frame as such
2. **Public figures have reduced privacy expectations** — But not zero
3. **Disclaimer is mandatory** — Every public figure analysis needs it
4. **Don't claim clinical authority** — Not psychologists
5. **Non-commercial academic use is safest** — But still needs disclaimer
6. **Goldwater Rule applies to clinical diagnosis** — Not typology (but be careful)
7. **Private individuals require consent** — Different legal standard
