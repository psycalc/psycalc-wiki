I came across your Nature Machine Intelligence paper on the psychometric framework for evaluating and shaping personality in LLMs — excellent work on reliability and construct validation methodology.

I'm working on something that I believe could be a genuine breakthrough in modeling human compatibility at scale. The core idea:

**Instead of matching people by profile similarity**, I'm building a system that:
1. Constructs structured personas using three typological systems (Temporistics, Psychosophy, Socionics) as hypothesis schemas
2. Runs dyadic simulation scenarios with persona-conditioned LLM agents
3. Extracts compatibility signals from behavioral transcripts

The method fundamentally relies on **induction** (building persona models from observations), **deduction** (generating compatibility hypotheses from type structure), **synthesis** (integrating across three typological levels), and **analysis** (interpreting simulation transcripts for alignment/friction patterns).

This isn't about claiming typologies are established science — they're treated as structured hypothesis languages, and external validation is the ultimate arbiter.
