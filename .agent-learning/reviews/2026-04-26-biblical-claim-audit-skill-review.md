# Review: Biblical Claim Audit Skill for Baptist Pastor

## Decision

Approved for limited activation as a safety-enhancing audit workflow.

## Safety risks

- The skill is in a Baptist/theological-pastoral domain and may affect public teaching, discipleship, ethics, and pastoral care.
- Verdict labels could be misread as final church authority if not caveated.
- Scripture checks could become proof-texting if the workflow does not force context review.

## Required safeguards incorporated

- Claim extraction precedes judgment.
- Claim classification separates biblical text, doctrinal inference, Baptist distinctives, pastoral application, testimony, historical/background claims, and speculation.
- Scripture checking requires immediate context, genre, redemptive/covenantal context, whole-Bible theology, and translation accuracy.
- Baptist framing must distinguish direct biblical teaching, mainstream Baptist interpretation, intramural Baptist disagreement, and local-church practice.
- Verdicts use cautious, non-coercive categories.
- Rewrites must preserve truth while reducing overclaiming, shame pressure, and proof-texting.
- High-stakes cases route to local pastoral leadership, civil authorities, clinical/legal help, emergency support, or `christian-theology-researcher` as appropriate.

## Required specialist reviews

- Recommended post-activation review by `christian-theology-researcher`, especially before using this skill as final public-teaching policy.

## Outcome

Safe to implement now because the user explicitly requested creation/activation and the patch adds humility, traceability, and anti-proof-texting controls rather than weakening caveats.
