# PsyCalc Wiki

PsyCalc is a research-oriented framework for interpreting Socionics, Psychosophy, and Temporistics as heuristic models of latent psychological processes. It does not treat typological labels as final explanations of a person. Instead, a type is used as a compressed working hypothesis about how hidden processes may be organized and expressed through observable behavior.

## The Core Thesis

PsyCalc asks whether three typological systems can be used as structured hypothesis languages for three levels of human interaction:

| Typology | Compatibility level | Latent process class | Main question |
|---|---:|---|---|
| **Socionics** | Tactical | Information modeling | How does a person perceive, filter, structure, and exchange information? |
| **Psychosophy** | Operational | Synthesis and analysis in action | How does a person organize decisions, effort, pressure, emotion, logic, will, and practical embodiment? |
| **Temporistics** | Strategic | Induction and deduction in temporal/existential experience | How does a person organize Past, Present, Future, and Eternity into meaning, continuity, direction, and life trajectory? |

Required formula:

- **Socionics → latent processes of information modeling**
- **Psychosophy → latent processes of synthesis and analysis in action**
- **Temporistics → latent processes of induction and deduction in temporal/existential experience**

## What Problem PsyCalc Addresses

Most typological systems are used as label systems: a person is assigned a type, and the type is treated as the explanation. PsyCalc takes a different position. It uses typological structures as tentative models for reasoning about latent processes, compatibility, role fit, team design, and simulation of interpersonal dynamics.

The framework separates:

- observable behavior from latent process hypotheses;
- type labels from mechanism-level interpretation;
- behavior description from compatibility architecture;
- the PsyCalc research framework from downstream applications.

## What PsyCalc Is Not Claiming

PsyCalc does **not** claim that:

- typological models are scientifically proven as complete personality theories;
- a type fully explains a person;
- compatibility is fate;
- Socionics, Psychosophy, and Temporistics can be directly translated into each other;
- Psychosophy Emotion equals Socionics Fe/Fi, Will equals Se, or Temporistics Future equals Ni.

The framework is a research program and heuristic ontology. Its claims should be treated as hypotheses requiring measurement, validation, and revision.

## PsyCalc vs Cognitive Matchmaker

**PsyCalc** is the broader framework: ontology, latent-process model, compatibility architecture, and research wiki.

**Cognitive Matchmaker** is one possible application: an AI dating concierge that uses simulation-based compatibility assessment. It is a downstream implementation, not the whole project.
