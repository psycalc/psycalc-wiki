## Core Claims Found

### What the steering wheel is

The Kalinauskas text says that the steering wheel is a structure used in Socionics to help perception, self/other TIM identification, and tracking how type-related elements are interconnected.

Kalinauskas links the steering wheel to the **method of qualitative structures** (`метод качественных структур`, MKS), described as a way of seeing a whole through qualitative aspects/functions rather than through parts.

### Relation to Model A

The dedicated site explicitly contrasts the steering wheel with Model A and says the site examines the steering-wheel model, not Model A. Therefore the safest classification is:

- Socionics school-specific / authorial interpretive schema;
- Model-A-adjacent, because it works with TIMs and information aspects;
- not the canonical Model A structure and not a replacement for Model A.

### Eight steering-wheel labels

The `shturval.me/select/` page presents eight labeled positions:

| Label | Russian Name | Short Meaning in Source |
|-------|--------------|-------------------------|
| I | Хорошо | principle of existence; level of personality organization |
| -I | Плохо | ignoring zone; minus-values |
| II | Надо | creativity; level of functioning |
| -II | Не надо | norms and behavioral stereotypes |
| III | Проблемы | self-assertion principle; least-resistance place |
| -III | Решения | self-realization zone; protection of self-esteem |
| IV | Хочу | place-evaluation principle; motives; suggestive zone |
| -IV | Боюсь | fear zone; suspiciousness/anxiety themes |

The source explains I as the sphere of confidence and existential self-support; II as functioning, creativity, risk, and demonstration of what one can do; III as self-evaluation, problem-zone, and psychological vulnerability; IV as suggestive openness, motives, and external influence.

### Opposite-type emphasis

The Kalinauskas excerpt gives special importance to the opposite type. In the Don Quixote / Balzac example, the opposite type is described as offering a “fullness of description” and as complementary in an informational sense. Kalinauskas even states that he considers the so-called opposite types to be the “real complementing” ones in that sense.

This diverges from mainstream Socionics language, where duality is usually the canonical complementary relation. Treat this as an authorial/school-specific claim.

### Use in relations

The `shturval.me/relat/` page uses the wheel to map standard Socionics relations such as identity, duality, mirror, activation, semi-duality, kindred, mirage, business, super-ego, conflict, quasi-identity, supervision, and benefit/order relations.

The Kalinauskas excerpt similarly shows a structure of interquadral relations from the base TIM: self, dual, mirror, activator, social order, kindred, semi-dual, controller, mirage, business, receiver, supervisee, complete opposite, super-ego, quasi-identity, and conflict.

## PsyCalc Source-Status Notes

- Treat the steering wheel as a **Socionics-school / authorial interpretive model**.
- Do not present it as universally accepted Socionics core theory.
- Do not present it as a replacement for Model A.
- Do not present “opposite type as true complement” as standard Socionics consensus.
- Do not treat resonance, suggestibility, or “speaking the language of eight types” claims as empirically validated.
- Use it as a conceptual map for tactical information-modeling hypotheses only.
