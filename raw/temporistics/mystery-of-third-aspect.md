# The Mystery of the Third Aspect

**By Dr. Radut**

This article was conceived to reveal several very important ideas, as I see them. It is designed for three purposes (and to delight you with pictures).

**Goal 1. ATTRACTION**

If you are only vaguely familiar with Temporistics, or have stumbled upon this completely by accident without even knowing Psychosophy (the Temporistics model is fully derived from Psychosophy, the only difference being the content of the aspects), I would like you to still be able to understand what is being discussed, become interested, and find benefit for yourself.

**Goal 2. UNDERSTANDING**

It seems to me extremely important to reveal the mechanism of how the third aspect works. Where all these difficulties and problems associated with it come from. Understanding is a stepping stone before application. One should not skip over it.

**Goal 3. PRACTICE**

Any theory has little meaning if you cannot apply what you know in practice. Therefore, a significant part of what is written is dedicated to making our third painful aspect work better, unfold, and provide energy, fulfillment, and joy of life. Moreover — it is precisely in our painful aspect, like a treasure chest, that the mystery of our destiny lies.

# PROLOGUE: Remembering Temporistics

Past, present, future, and eternity are the four spheres of time, each filled with its own values, its own resources, its own questions, and sometimes its own problems. Need I say that for a personality to be balanced, a person must have harmony, interconnection, and fulfillment in each time sphere, and if I may speak in Temporistics language — in each temporal aspect.

It is important for a person to have a relationship with each of the four times — and "eternity" in this case is also a category of time, even if timeless.

If a person ignores one of the times — let's say closes their eyes to the past or does not want to think about the future, ignores eternity, or drops out of the present — their personality will be severely deformed and cause suffering, both to the person themselves and to those around them.

Under what circumstances might a person develop the desire to deny the importance of any particular time sphere? Temporistics tells us that each person has a certain internal hierarchy of time.

For example, my hierarchy is: past in first place, eternity in second, present in third, future in fourth. My friend's hierarchy: past-present-future-eternity. My brother's hierarchy: eternity-past-present-future. You also have your own hierarchy. Thus, there are 24 different combinations of time perception and relationship to it.

Of course, one should not think that hierarchy denotes a simple order of preference — things are somewhat more complicated here. More details can be found in other articles and on the site, but here I simply want to remind that:

- The **first aspect** (first position in the hierarchy) determines a person's goal in life, their sphere of realization. This is the "target" aspect.
- The **second aspect** is the sphere of the best application of a person's efforts and energy, their unquenchable interest and joy. This is the "creative" aspect.
- The **third aspect** is a person's wound and the criterion for their goal, the main compass of their life, subtle, vulnerable, and sensitive. It is precisely about this that this article is written. This is the "painful" aspect.
- The **fourth aspect** is not very significant for the person themselves, low-energy, used as a resource for higher aspects. It is also called the "blind" aspect.

> "The target aspect looks at its time and says — 'This is my task.'
>
> The painful aspect looks and says — 'This is my problem.'
>
> The blind aspect reports — 'This is a task, this is a problem, but... not mine.'
>
> And between the target and painful aspects is the creative one.
>
> Looking at its time, it thinks — 'This is a riddle.'
>
> A. Latyshev

Let's return to the question of under what circumstances a person might be tempted to ignore a particular time. Since the two upper aspects (target and creative) are strong and give a person the opportunity to feel their capabilities within these time spheres, it is understandable that a person will never ignore them, otherwise they would lose their main supports. There is also no temptation to ignore the time in the fourth aspect (resource) — in fact, it often switches off by itself and this area in a person's life remains empty.

Usually this happens when insufficient energy flows to the third aspect, but this is not the result of the person's choice within this time. For example, my brother, when things get difficult for him regarding his 3rd (painful) present, dismissively speaks about the future, saying that no future will come in principle, that "there is simply no 'future'." By the way, this greatly traumatizes his wife with a 3rd future, but that is another story.

So we have arrived at an important point. An enormous, all-consuming desire to deny the value and significance for oneself of a particular time sphere is experienced precisely regarding the third aspect, or more precisely, the time that stands in this position.

- People with Past in the third position relate to themselves as harsh Critics, and sometimes most want to forget everything that has happened to them.
- People with Present in the third position are called Outcasts, and sometimes most want to escape from the realities that surround them.
- People with Future in the third position feel like Stowaways on the ship of destiny, and try not to think about "where they are sailing," since they are not sure they have the right to "change course."
- People with a third Eternity often behave like Philistines, since sometimes it seems easier to them (as it appears) to ignore everything that goes beyond the visible world and understandable categories.

Need I say that the third aspect and the time filling it must never be ignored, denied, or avoided? To my extreme amazement, I have also encountered the opinion that one should do everything according to their strong aspects, pay attention to them, and simply "shove" the third one aside.

This strategy, as I have already mentioned, is not just wrong — it leads to catastrophe at the level of psyche! With such an approach, the first, target aspect will become even more categorical, pushy, and harsh, because the person develops the illusion that they need to develop it precisely, since the target aspect is also a way to defend themselves (that is, their third, since self-esteem is tied to the third aspect for us). Thus, a person, using A. Afanasyev's apt expression, will be "pumping overdeveloped muscles," leaving wounded and weak what actually requires healing and development.

Once I analyzed the questionnaire of a person with 1st future and 3rd present who so greatly neglected his current life situation that a minimum of two-thirds of his attention and energy automatically went to the future. He lived and dreamed of his goals, plans, illusively believing that "happiness is ahead," while his present, naturally, represented a black hole. This is what deformation looks like.

The reverse deformation occurs in people with the arrangement — 1st present and 3rd future. A person with this type of time perception may not want to move forward and change something, because they do not want to face the pain and fears of their "triplet" — distrust of the future. In such a case, they will hold onto their place in the present, endure any humiliation and inconvenience (sometimes even violence in relationships), just to maintain the "status quo" — covering this behavioral model with the popular slogan about accepting life "as it is" and the ability to live "in the present." They truly know how to live in the present, they do not need to learn it specially. But they are afraid to move forward, and this is the area that needs their participation.

It is important to develop what is weak with you. The strong will take care of itself.

The strong aspects will not go anywhere anyway, they function even in sleep, even in an unconscious state, because an ocean of energy is given to them.

*I will reveal a secret: real development, real action, we can perform ONLY ACCORDING TO THE THIRD ASPECT.*

Everything else will only be an illusion of change, external thrashing. Regarding time, there is no other area for exercising our freedom except in the domain of the third (painful) aspect. If in other times we act REACTIVELY, that is, we react to existing stimuli, patterns, and representations, then in the third aspect we are PROACTIVE, meaning we can truly change something, engaging freedom of will.

I have already mentioned what effects occur when taking action according to the third aspect, but I will repeat: personality harmonizes, the person becomes more conscious and spontaneous, it is interesting to be around them, their company inspires, they get the opportunity to influence others and make them better, since they have themselves "removed the beam from their own eye." Such a person realizes themselves in the full sense of the word.

A person who correctly acts according to their third aspect attracts those people who complement them — but this is a topic for a separate article about how I found my husband through intertype relations.

So, to do something and especially to change something, one must first understand what it is and how it works. Let us examine in more detail the mechanism of how the third aspect functions and why we often simply want to run away from it.

# Part 1. Ominous. The Serpent's Intrigue

## Reality, Power, Vision

*"And the world split in half, the fracture smokes,*
*And blood flows — war comes, good against evil..."*
(c) Umurtman

The third aspect is the sphere of loneliness, fear, and a sense of divine abandonment.

Whether you are a believer, agnostic, or atheist, at certain moments of life you probably have a vague feeling of the world's, the universe's, God's, or Higher Reason's goodwill toward you.

Belief that God is good in general, and toward you in particular, is easy when it concerns the upper two aspects, but at the level of the third aspect this feeling malfunctions and the subconscious transmits the image of at best an "Indifferent God," if not a cruel and hostile one.

In the time that lives in our third aspect, we "see" ourselves as abandoned and alone, deprived of help and support, wandering in darkness. This image of oneself in hostile Past, Present, Future, or Eternity (depending on what is your third) comes to us irrationally, from within, which is why it is so easy to believe in this image, because it seems to be "programmed" in us.

For example, my friend with a painful Future (PNBV — Past-Present-Future-Eternity), may believe that God was good to her in the Past (looking back, she sees that everything that happened happened for good), and she can easily feel loved by Him in the Present. However, when it comes to making a serious decision — changing a situation (change is the psychological threshold after which the Future sphere begins), she can analyze various options indefinitely but decide nothing, because she has the feeling that in the Future she is alone, any mistake is fatal, and all responsibility lies with her.

Since it is impossible to calculate all risks, she will procrastinate and delay, trying to stay as long as possible in the love-filled and safe Present.

But the only thing that can truly help in such a situation is to take a "leap of faith" and step into the unknown.

For some, such problems are familiar; others suffer from entirely different difficulties...

Why does this happen?

## A Minute of Theory

Each time aspect can be defined as: real (present, eternity) or unreal (past, future), subject to control (present, future) and not subject to control (past, eternity), as well as abstract (eternity, future) and concrete (past, present).

Past — concrete, not subject to control, and unreal.
Present — concrete, subject to control, and real.
Future — abstract, subject to control, and unreal.
Eternity — abstract, not subject to control, and real.

Let us examine each of these terms in detail.

**What do "reality" and "unreality" mean?**

Real — means it actually exists and at this moment.
The Present and Eternity are real.

Unreal — means the time exists only as a projection, for example, in the form of a memory or fantasy. Past and Future are unreal.

"Past is ghostly because it no longer exists. Future is ghostly because it does not yet exist." (N. Berdyaev)

**What do "subject to control" and "not subject to control" mean?**

Subject to control — means depends on our actions, we can actively influence something. Present and Future are subject to control.

Not subject to control — means sovereign, independent, and exists on its own, moreover — actively influences us. Past and Eternity are not subject to control.

**What do "abstract" and "concrete" mean?**

Abstract — perceived through images, interpretations, vague pictures, representations.

Concrete — what has unique features and details, facts, what is visible. Past and Present are concrete; Future and Eternity are abstract.

**So what exactly is the problem with the third aspect?**

A large part of the difficulties of the third aspect are based on an interesting subconscious trap. Whatever time stands in your hierarchy at "level three," you can be sure that at this level you constantly hear lies from your inner... enemy.

Imagine that the very enemy of the human race, the serpent from the Garden of Eden, crawled into your own "Garden of Time" and chose one of the four trees growing there — the tree of past, present, future, or eternity. It not only built a nest on it and devours its fruits (I will tell you about these), but also hisses all kinds of lies into your ears, drowning out the quiet voice of truth.

**What does it lie about?**

The serpent — we will continue using this image — INVERTS the properties of time for us. About the real, it hisses that it is ghostly; about the subject to control, it whispers about the impossibility of influencing; about the unreal — it convinces that it exists; about the not subject to control — it suggests "go ahead, try." When it comes to "abstract" time, it demands to present facts and imposes non-existent details; and it blurs the concrete into vague images and whispers its interpretation of what was and what is.

It is important to understand that the serpent is our ENEMY.

- It has built its nest on the third tree in our Garden of Time.
- It lies to us absolutely about everything related to our painful time.
- It devours the fruit from this tree every time we believe it.

It is important to understand that it is precisely the serpent's lies that shackle us in invisible chains. It is because of these lies that the third aspect is felt as a domain of loneliness, darkness, and pain.

| Time | Lie | Truth |
|------|-----|-------|
| Past | Real / Subject to Control / Abstract | Unreal / Not Subject to Control / Concrete |
| Present | Unreal / Not Subject to Control / Abstract | Real / Subject to Control / Concrete |
| Future | Real / Not Subject to Control / Concrete | Unreal / Subject to Control / Abstract |
| Eternity | Unreal / Subject to Control / Concrete | Real / Not Subject to Control / Abstract |

To illustrate the subtle game of thrones that the serpent plays in the third aspect, I chose the house mottos from George Martin's book "A Song of Ice and Fire."

# THIRD PAST. The Critic

*"Fire and Blood" — the motto of House Targaryen*

**What does the past give us, or the fruits of strength**

Past, based on its true characteristics, is unreal, not subject to control, and concrete. The logical question arises: why do we need the past if it no longer exists in all its occurred details, and we cannot influence it? The point is that the past is the only source of experience and formation of our character. "What does not kill us makes us stronger" — this proverb is popular for good reason.

By integrating the diverse experience we have acquired in life, we acquire ourselves — transforming from who we are into who we can become. What we have lived through is our part — "my years, my wealth."

The connections we have acquired help us stand firm. The adventures we have experienced make us multifaceted. The suffering and losses we have endured make us more sensitive and compassionate. And the mistakes or sins we have committed teach us to stay on the right path and develop our spirit, if we have understood and comprehended them.

**What does the serpent lie about?**

What happens in the third aspect of the Critic (a person with a painful past)? If the serpent has climbed precisely this tree, such a person tends to believe the lie that the past is:

1. Real
2. Subject to control
3. Abstract

The reality of anything allows one to interact with it and perceive what actually is, expanding and deepening one's understanding of it. Subject to control — allows one to influence, and abstractness allows one to fill the "form" with any content.

Under the weight of the serpent's lies, a person with a third past becomes hostage to constant attempts to change what was — the past seems to be occurring right now (lie about the past's reality), and there is no ability to let go and process it (lie about subject to control). The Critic also falls under the influence of their own negative interpretations, because they trust the images of the past that have formed in their head (lie about abstractness) — instead of looking at what actually was, they look at what *seems* to them.

The Critic realistically and very vividly lives through the pictures of the past, as if "diving there" and repeating again and again, despite the fact that they may have incorrectly remembered the situation, misunderstood it, missed all the details — oh no, the past in the Critic's perception is REAL, meaning it exists here and now in full.

The instilled sense of past's controllability makes the Critic indulge in fantasies about what they could have changed in the past.

Yes, they could have looked more broadly at any moment of their life and evaluated it objectively, but the false abstractness of the past does not allow them to do this, and they prefer to keep vague pictures of the past and believe their emotional imprints — and it is from these pictures that their "self-image" is formed.

**The fruit from the tree of the Past is STRENGTH.**

The lie of the third aspect that the past is real, subject to control, and abstract makes it impossible for the Critic to do a very important thing. With such an approach, the owner of the third past cannot comprehend, evaluate, and integrate the events of their life, transforming them into their qualities. Thus, the serpent devours the fruit of STRENGTH. That strength that would belong to this person by right and was the legitimate result of their life experience.

A person deprived of inner strength is like a city without walls, they lose an important part of their boundaries and sense of security (the second step of Maslow's pyramid). They are deprived of peace.

Also, the serpent's lies at the level of the third past generate a false emotion: unending SORROW for what was.

*The reasons why this emotion is false and what the true emotion is will be discussed in the second part of this article.*

**The nest that the serpent built on the tree of the Past is called: GUILT.**

It is precisely the endless sense of guilt that makes a person constantly remember negative moments of the past and mentally redo them, scolding and accusing themselves. To scold and accuse essentially nobody — the person who committed the misdeed no longer exists, since they themselves have moved forward along life's path and changed in the process — but the Critic perceives it differently. The Critic, under the influence of lies in the third aspect, shifts the focus of their real responsibility to the realm of the unreal, uncontrollable, and abstract, delighting the serpent and growing a shell of secrecy. They are ashamed of their past actions and do not want others to know "who they really are."

The Critic usually does not believe that they change. They think they are only accumulating new events, but they do not believe in real changes at the level of their personality, it seems to them that transformation ("metanoia" — (Greek) change of mind, repentance) is impossible. They are still the same as when they committed something, from their point of view, wrong.

One of my friends with a third past (VNPU — Eternity-Present-Past-Present), a psychologist suggested she imagine herself as a child, open her "inner child" — my friend later recounted that, looking into herself, she experienced burning hatred for that little girl inside: — "She is not growing!" — she angrily cried to the psychologist — "She simply does not want to grow!"

**The serpent's tail has drawn its fiery trail between a person's "qualities" and "awareness of change."**

Such a person needs to go through the fire of transformation to become free. They should see their past as a series of events in which they formed as a personality, shift the point of their responsibility to the present. Find the answer to the question "Who am I?" and realize essential changes.

> "I am of dragon blood and I should be strong. They should see fire in my eyes, not tears."
>
> Daenerys (George Martin. A Storm of Swords)

# THIRD FUTURE. The Stowaway

*"Winter is Coming" — the motto of House Stark*

**What does the future give us, or the fruits of hope**

The future gives us a path and a sense of movement, dynamics. Development and expansion of our capabilities, novelty. If the past helps our personal development and changes in our character, then the future helps change circumstances and develop externally. Forward movement is aided by hope that things will be better than now. This is the lighthouse shining in the distance and showing the way.

What is the serpent's lie about the future?

It suggests to the Stowaway (a person with a third aspect of future) that the future is:

1. Real
2. Not subject to control
3. Concrete

How does this affect them? Since the future is perceived in the present through dynamics — a person with a third future has unwanted dynamics instantly drawing in all details (lie about concreteness) and fixing it in their imagination (lie about reality) the unwanted future, making this future practically inevitable (lie about not subject to control).

The Stowaway suffers from the fact that something inevitable, horrible, and merciless is inexorably approaching from the future — the picture of the unwanted future is constantly before their eyes.

Moreover, believing the serpent's lies about the future's not subject to control ("I cannot"), such a person feeds the cold-blooded enemy the fruits of hope and themselves becomes engulfed by a false emotion of the future — regret.

The Stowaway regrets what has not yet happened, because they are convinced that the negative picture of the future is real and concrete (for example, "I will die at eighty in a cold bed, alone, with a glass of water in trembling hands"), and they themselves cannot change anything. From this truly comes hopelessness, and inside like a light goes out.

**The fruit from the tree of the Future is HOPE.**

> Alena (PNBV — Past-Present-Future-Eternity):
>
> "...The feeling that regarding the future I am not capable of doing something important and truly good, suitable. Something I would, say, be proud of or be satisfied with, or at least considered correct... And in such moments I seem to tell myself: well, just live for today... Get up, work, eat, and sleep... Anyway, 'supposedly' I cannot do anything needed (cannot do)... And in such moments I close my eyes to the future and return to where it is comfortable for me! (For example, to the bustle of the present, where there is a feeling of action)."

**The nest that the serpent built on the tree of the Future is called CONTROL.**

The problem with the third Future is that due to anxiety before the unknown, the Stowaway wants to control everything. And everything that is controlled loses spontaneity and flexibility, freezes, and ceases to be natural — so people with a third future suffer, frozen at the threshold of their development, and only a few of them decide to take a step.

The Stowaway constantly wants to change their future ("turn the wheel" in the right direction) — this is their urgent need, the criterion for the correctness of their life. However, since the future can be changed only by making and implementing a decision, and the Stowaway does not trust their ability to choose from many options the correct one (do not confuse with the third will in Psychosophy, where the problem is making a decision; here it is choosing the correct one). They often hesitate, delay, waver, and doubt — sit on two chairs, getting neither in the end. They want everything to resolve itself or for some unambiguously weighty arguments to appear that would allow them to prefer one option over another; they find it difficult to imagine that the future is unknown to everyone (while they have the illusion that others know something they do not), and absolutely every decision carries risk...

Because a person with a third future feels that in the future they are alone, they naturally need to foresee everything.

Also, they desperately want to secure *belonging* (the third step of Maslow's pyramid) to certain groups of people, so that in the future they will not end up alone (so they are not "thrown off the ship") — thus, the Stowaway often fears to oppose others, and sometimes backs down where it is required to state their disagreement with something generally accepted.

**In the Stowaway's soul, the serpent's tail has drawn its icy trail between "options" and "decision."**

Such a person needs to break the ice of anxiety to become free. Under the ice, living springs pulse, but one must still make one's way to them, gradually moving forward.

# THIRD PRESENT. The Outcast

*"Hear Me Roar" — the motto of House Lannister*

**What does the present give us, or the fruit of confidence**

The present is perceived through interaction. This can be interaction with people or with nature and other surroundings, but there is no other means of perceiving the present. Reality is given to us in sensations and we perceive it only through contact with it. For a person in the present, it is so important to find their own worthy "place," and with it, recognition of a certain role/status/authority.

The serpent's lie about the Present in the third aspect sounds like this. The present:

1. Is unreal
2. Is not subject to control
3. Is abstract

This means that a person with a third present, believing the above, lives as if "for show," constantly looking around searching for something "real," "worthy," "deserving"... (lie about unreality). They do not think they can significantly influence what is happening (lie about not subject to control) and they see not what is around them in reality, but the images and interpretations of what is happening that the serpent obligingly feeds them (lie about abstractness) — and this, as a rule, is not what is in their heart.

Thus, a person becomes an Outcast.
